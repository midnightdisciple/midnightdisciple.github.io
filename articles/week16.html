The Myth of Satisfaction

Anything.

That’s what I’m supposed to be doing.

“Anything”, in this case, meaning anything except what I’d actually like to do.

“What I’d like to do”, in this case, meaning anything except what I know I should do.

Sometimes, we want the wrong things.

“Wanting the wrong things”, in this case, meaning either wanting something that’s bad for you, or wanting something you don’t want.

“Wanting something you don’t want”, in this case, meaning the desire for something that is, by it’s nature, unfulfillable.

“Unfulfillable Desires”, in this case, meaning…

The search for a summer that’s always a little less fun, a little shorter, and a little more normal than you thought it would be, than you remember it being last time.

The craving for a cookie that always leaves your mouth a little dryer, your stomach a little sicker, and the craving a little stronger than you thought they would be, than you remember them being last time.

The itch for a high that always leaves you questioning when the last time you managed to scratch it was, or why scratching it always makes the itch come back stronger.

Satisfaction is a myth created by corporations as a marketing gimmick to sell you on the concept of your own incompleteness, and thereby your own completion.

Have a good night.
