Give It Up

My wound existed before me, I was born to embody it.

— Joe Bousquet —

My older sister doesn’t know how much she means to me. This is my fault. The times I have said this to her, it is always undermined by my lack of confidence.

My <target of affection> is neglected via self dismissal.

My past haunts me in the form of repeated experiences. I seek out the same stimulus in hopes of redeeming the past.

My <maintained identification> self perpetuates.

I am misunderstood and alone.

My true self remains unformed or undiscovered.

I hated writing this almost as much as I wish I could hate myself. It feels almost as meaningless as the concept of identity. I am back home and feel utterly stuck. These articles which gave me a sense of purpose for a time now seem quite bland or uninteresting.
