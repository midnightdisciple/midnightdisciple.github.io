My Story

Father’s words lack substance
His first conceited crime
Not granting me dominion
Cast down before my time

Mortality’s affliction
In mirror on the wall
His twisted maze is fiction
He laughs above it all

Callous breaks the living
Death’s shadow looms tall
Darkness reigns, takes everything
Transcendence fates the fall

It starts and starts but doesn’t stop
It feels beyond sensation
Through highs it climbs and lows it drops
To remake all creation

That which lies in emptiness
And doesn’t seek to find
Sees both sighs and shrieks as melodies
Perfectly defined




Context For Curious Readers

This poem, written about my life, uses the biblical figure Lucifer as a device to explore demonic influence/possession as a psychological metaphor, as well as comment on the nature of existence.

Stanzas are, in order:

  • I am (from the perspective of me and Lucifer)
  • I am not (from the perspective of me, unconscious of Lucifer’s influence)
  • I am not that I am (from a mixed perspective, semi-conscious, ripe with madness)
  • I am that I am (fragmented and empty perspective, madness manifest)
  • Neither I am, nor am I not
