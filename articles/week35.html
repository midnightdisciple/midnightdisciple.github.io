“I want to see the truth” is a very messed up phrase. The implication being that the truth is anything other than readily apparent. When you want to see, instead of seeing, you conjure an image utterly transparent.

The trick is not to see, but to stare. Only when you stare does your gaze pierce rather than reflect. For when we seek to see, we project
