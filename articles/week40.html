The Power of Self Respect

I love the sound that chalk makes on a whiteboard.
Or the little white flecks it throws around.

I also love the sounds a woman makes when she’s aroused.
Or the little trembles in her body that reward my efforts.

I love the smell of gasoline, and many other things others find grotesque or strange.
Or maybe I just love the fact I have certain things to myself.

I love that when I see the truth for what it is, it always upsets me.
Or the fact that my broken heart mends only to break more fully.

I love that this poem doesn’t exist, is merely a collection of letters on a page.
Or the self-realization that there isn’t one.
